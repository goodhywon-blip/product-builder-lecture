Notes about name spellings, pronunciation, and usage.

How to choose a spelling

  • Hangul spellings vary by preference and region.
  • Shorter spellings often look more natural on name tags.
  • When in doubt, use the spelling a Korean speaker would read comfortably.

Common patterns

  • Long “a” sounds often map to “에이”.
  • “th” can vary between ㅅ and ㅌ depending on preference.
  • Final consonants may be softened to read smoothly in Hangul.

Corrections

If you prefer a different spelling, submit a community suggestion on the homepage so it can be reviewed.
